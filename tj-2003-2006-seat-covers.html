What Makes This Year Range Different

The 2003–2006 run includes two models: the standard two-door TJ and the LJ (Wrangler Unlimited) with its 15-inch longer wheelbase and a proper rear bench seat with real legroom. This is the last hurrah of the TJ generation before the JK took over in 2007. Front seat covers share the same pattern between TJ and LJ — the front buckets are identical between them. Rear seat covers require LJ-specific sizing because the LJ rear bench is significantly larger than the two-door TJ's cramped jump seats.

No side-curtain airbags on any 2003–2006 TJ or LJ trim means airbag compatibility is not a safety concern — a key advantage over later JK and JL generation shopping.

Fitment Notes for the 2003–2006 TJ/LJ

  • No side-curtain airbags on any 2003–2006 TJ or LJ trim — airbag compatibility is not a concern.
  • TJ and LJ front seats are the same — front covers are interchangeable between two-door TJ and four-door LJ.
  • LJ rear bench is significantly larger than the standard TJ rear jump seats — always order LJ-specific rear covers if you have an LJ.
  • Bartact splits TJ production between 1997–2002 and 2003–2006 — order the correct year range for your specific TJ.
  • Many high-mileage TJs and LJs have aftermarket seats — confirm your seat model before ordering.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best seat covers for a 2003–2006 Jeep Wrangler TJ/LJ?

Bartact is the top overall pick — cut and hand-sewn in the USA with 2003–2006 TJ/LJ-specific patterning, mil-spec MOLLE webbing, and Cordura nylon. For Amazon options, Rough Country offers TJ/LJ-compatible neoprene covers at a mid-range price.

What is the difference between the TJ and the LJ?

The LJ (Wrangler Unlimited) has a 15-inch longer wheelbase and a larger rear bench seat with real legroom. Front seats are identical between TJ and LJ. If you have an LJ, verify that any rear seat covers are sized for the LJ's larger rear bench, not the cramped TJ jump seats.

Do 2003–2006 TJ/LJ Wranglers have side airbags?

No. The 2003–2006 Jeep Wrangler TJ and LJ did not have side-curtain airbags on any trim level. Airbag compatibility is not a safety requirement for seat covers on these vehicles.

Can I use 1997–2002 TJ covers on my 2003–2006 TJ?

Not with precision. Jeep made minor seat changes across the TJ run. Bartact patterns separately for each year range to ensure correct fitment. Order the 2003–2006 pattern for your late TJ.
